Web Animation & Motion Design Consulting

Put animation front and center.

Operating systems have invested mightily in motion design since the early 90s, and now the web is ready to embrace the same principles. Since Google highlighted the importance of meaningful animations and transitions in their Material Design documentation, web-facing companies like IBM have been following suite. The time has come to add animation to the tools we use to build the web.

But it's the wild west out there. While game and app developers seem to have made great progress in animating interfaces and orchestrating interactions, the web development community has lagged behind. What's more, those communities that have mastered animation do not publish their findings openly, often keeping them under lock and key at large software companies, or learning from mentors at small shops.

It's dangerous to go alone. Take me with you.

I've been working with browser-first animation before it was cool since day one, writing articles and giving talks both on theory and implementation. My work has landed me in the W3C's CSS Working Group, where I argue advocate on behalf of developers and designers in the field. And I can help you, too. Let me…

  • …spot interactions with your UI where transitions and animations can ease cognitive load.
  • …help your engineers incorporate animation callbacks and fallbacks into your site's frontend infrastructure.
  • …contribute quality CSS and JavaScript to your codebase.
  • …evaluate existing animation for performance and effectiveness.
  • …train your team to do all these things for themselves.

With equal experience on both the use of animation to further the user experience and the technical challenges of implementing complex animations, you can ask for no better guide into this budding field.
